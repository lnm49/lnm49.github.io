Introduction

Spain had a very powerful empire in the 16th and 17th century before falling behind to Britain, France, and Germany in economic and political power.

They remained neutral in both world wars, and suffered a lot through a civil war of their own from 1936-1939. The country went through a peaceful transition to democracy after the death of dictator Franco in 1975

They also went through a period of rapid economic modernization as Spain joined the EU in 1986. Spain is currently the Europeâ€™s fourth largest economy. The country has continued to face pressure in recent years due to the independence movement in Catalonia.

Geography

Location

Spain is located in Southwestern Europe, it borders the Mediterranean Sea, North Atlantic Ocean, Bay of Biscay, and the Pyrenees Mountains.

Land Boundaries

Spain has five border countries which include:

  • Andorra
  • France
  • Gibraltar
  • Portugal
  • Morocco

Spanning a total of 1,952.7km.

Current Issues

An issue they are currently facing is pollution of the Mediterranean Sea from raw sewage and offshore production of oil and gas. Another issue is the quality and quantity of water nationwide, air pollution and deforestation.

People and Society

Population

Spain has a population of over 47 million people. The main urban areas are Madrid with 6.669 million people, Barcelona has 5.624 million, and Valencia with 835,000 people.

Ethnic Groups

The three main ethnic groups in the country are:

Languages

Although a few languages are spoken throughout different regions, the main one is Castilian Spanish with a 74% of the population speaking it as a main language. Then there is Catalan with 17%, Galician 7%, and finally Basque with a 2%.

Government

Government Type

Spain has a parliamentary constitutional monarchy, which means they have a King
